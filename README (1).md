## 📌 Overview

A leading retail company wanted to better understand its customers' shopping behavior to improve sales, customer satisfaction, and long-term loyalty. This project analyzes the company's consumer behavior dataset to answer the overarching business question:

> **"How can the company leverage consumer shopping data to identify trends, improve customer engagement, and optimize marketing and product strategies?"**

The project covers the complete data analytics pipeline — data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), SQL-based business queries, and interactive dashboard creation using Power BI — to understand revenue trends, customer segments, and product performance and support data-driven decision-making.

## ⚙️ Project Workflow

### 🔹 1. Data Cleaning & Preprocessing (Python)
- Loaded the dataset using `pandas` and explored it with `df.info()` and `df.describe()`
- Handled 37 missing values in `review_rating` using **median imputation by product category**
- Standardized all column names to `snake_case`
- Verified `discount_applied` and `promo_code_used` were redundant and dropped `promo_code_used`

### 🔹 2. Feature Engineering
- Created `age_group` by binning customer ages into quartiles: *Young Adult, Adult, Middle-aged, Senior*
- Derived `purchase_frequency_days` by mapping purchase frequency labels (e.g., Weekly → 7, Monthly → 30, Annually → 365) to numeric day values

### 🔹 3. Database Integration
- Connected Python to **PostgreSQL** using `SQLAlchemy`
- Loaded the cleaned DataFrame into a `customer` table for SQL-based analysis

### 🔹 4. SQL-Based Business Analysis
Wrote and executed 10 structured SQL queries in PostgreSQL to answer key business questions:

| # | Business Question |
|---|---|
| 1 | Total revenue generated by male vs. female customers |
| 2 | Customers who used a discount but still spent above the average purchase amount |
| 3 | Top 5 products by average review rating |
| 4 | Average purchase amount: Standard vs. Express shipping |
| 5 | Average spend & total revenue: Subscribers vs. non-subscribers |
| 6 | Top 5 products with the highest percentage of discounted purchases |
| 7 | Customer segmentation into New, Returning, and Loyal |
| 8 | Top 3 most purchased products within each category |
| 9 | Whether repeat buyers (>5 purchases) are more likely to subscribe |
| 10 | Revenue contribution by age group |

<details>
<summary><strong>Sample query — Customer Segmentation (click to expand)</strong></summary>

```sql
WITH customer_type AS (
    SELECT customer_id, previous_purchases,
    CASE 
        WHEN previous_purchases = 1 THEN 'New'
        WHEN previous_purchases BETWEEN 2 AND 10 THEN 'Returning'
        ELSE 'Loyal'
    END AS customer_segment
    FROM customer
)
SELECT customer_segment, COUNT(*) AS "Number of Customers"
FROM customer_type
GROUP BY customer_segment;
```

</details>

Full query set available in [`customer_behavior_sql_queries.sql`](./sql/customer_behavior_sql_queries.sql).

### 🔹 5. Data Visualization (Power BI)

- Built donut and bar charts for revenue/sales by category and age group

## 📊 Key Insights

- 💰 **Revenue Gap by Gender** — Male customers generated **$157,890** vs. **$75,191** from female customers (2.1x higher)
- 👥 **Strong Loyalty** — **80%** of customers (3,116 of 3,900) fall into the **Loyal** segment
- 🚚 **Shipping Behavior** — Express shipping customers spend **$60.48** on average vs. **$58.46** for Standard
- 📈 **Top Revenue Segment** — **Young Adults** generate the highest revenue at **$62,143**
- 🔁 **Repeat Buyers** — **958** customers made more than 5 purchases; only a portion of these are subscribers
- 🏷️ **Discount Dependency** — Products like **Hat (50%)**, **Sneakers (49.66%)**, and **Coat (49.07%)** rely heavily on discounted purchases
- ⭐ **Top Rated Products** — Gloves (3.86), Sandals (3.84), Boots (3.82)
- 📬 **Subscribers vs. Non-Subscribers** — Non-subscribers generate more total revenue ($170,436 vs. $62,645), despite subscribers being a smaller group (1,053 vs. 2,847)
